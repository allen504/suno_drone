Module 3: Electronics & Wiring

This module builds the electrical foundation students need before they touch the drone’s real hardware. Using breadboards, power sources, and measuring tools, students learn how circuits behave in the real world.

Main curriculum weeks: 3 (Introduction & DC Circuits), 4 (Ohm’s Law & Measurement), 5 (Series & Parallel Circuits), 6 (Power Sources & Batteries), 7 (Voltage Drop & Real-World Effects)

Week 3 – Introduction & DC Circuit Slides

Students are introduced to basic circuit concepts like voltage, current, and resistance, and see how these ideas show up in everyday electronics.

Goals

  • Orient students to the course and lab expectations.
  • Understand what voltage, current, and resistance mean.
  • Recognize simple DC circuit symbols and diagrams.

In-Class Activities

  • Slide-based introduction to DC circuits with real-life examples.
  • Discuss batteries, LEDs, and other familiar components.
  • Short prediction questions about what will happen in simple circuits.

Why This Matters

These concepts are the vocabulary of electronics. Students will revisit them repeatedly while wiring and debugging the drone.

Week 4 – Ohm’s Law & Measurement

Students learn Ohm’s Law and practice using a multimeter to measure voltage, current, and resistance in a real circuit.

Goals

  • Understand and apply Ohm’s Law (V = I × R).
  • Use a multimeter safely and correctly.
  • Compare predicted values with actual measurements.

In-Class Activities

  • Build simple resistor circuits on a breadboard.
  • Measure voltage across and current through components.
  • Practice swapping resistor values to see the effect on current.

Why This Matters

Being able to measure and verify what a circuit is doing is essential for diagnosing issues later in the drone build.

Week 5 – Series & Parallel Circuits

Students discover how combining resistors in series and parallel changes total resistance and how voltage and current distribute across a circuit.

Goals

  • Differentiate between series and parallel connections.
  • Predict total resistance for both types of networks.
  • Relate circuit configuration to brightness or behavior of loads.

In-Class Activities

  • Build both series and parallel LED/resistor circuits.
  • Measure currents and voltages in each configuration.
  • Compare observations to theoretical calculations.

Why This Matters

Real drones contain lots of series and parallel paths. Understanding how they behave prevents wiring mistakes that could damage components.

Week 6 – Power Sources & Batteries

Students look more closely at batteries and power supplies, learning about capacity, internal resistance, and why power management matters.

Goals

  • Understand basic battery specs (voltage, capacity, C-rating).
  • Discuss internal resistance and how it affects performance.
  • Connect batteries safely in simple test circuits.

In-Class Activities

  • Examine labels and datasheets for different batteries.
  • Compare how different sources behave under load.
  • Discuss LiPo safety and classroom handling rules.

Why This Matters

The drone depends on safe, reliable power. Misunderstanding batteries can lead to brownouts, crashes, or safety hazards.

Week 7 – Voltage Drop & Real-World Effects

Students see how wires, connections, and real components introduce voltage drops and losses that don’t show up in idealized diagrams.

Goals

  • Observe voltage drops along wires and across components.
  • Recognize why long or thin wires can cause problems.
  • Connect theory to the messy reality of physical circuits.

In-Class Activities

  • Build circuits that intentionally show noticeable voltage drop.
  • Measure voltages at multiple points and compare to expectations.
  • Discuss how these issues could affect a drone in flight.

Why This Matters

Understanding real-world losses prepares students to route wires intelligently and diagnose subtle power problems in their drone.
