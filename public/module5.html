Module 5: Assembly

Assembly is where everything comes together: motors, sensors, radio, power, and frame. Students integrate hardware into a complete system and prepare for flight.

Main curriculum weeks: 18 (Sensor Integration), 19 (Radio Module Setup), 20 (Power Wiring & Safety), 25 (Bench Flight & Safety Check), 26 (Maiden Flight)

Week 18 – Sensor Integration

Students install and wire the main IMU (e.g., MPU6050) and ensure that the sensor is mounted in the correct orientation on the drone.

Goals

  • Mount the IMU securely to minimize vibration.
  • Wire sensor power, ground, and data lines correctly.
  • Verify communication between Arduino and sensor.

In-Class Activities

  • Physically mount the IMU to the frame using screws or tape.
  • Wire the sensor to the Arduino and double-check connections.
  • Upload a test sketch to confirm data is streaming correctly.

Why This Matters

The IMU is the drone’s sense of balance. Poor mounting or wiring can cause unstable flight even if the code is perfect.

Week 19 – Radio Module Setup

Students install and configure the wireless radio module that links the drone to its controller (e.g., an NRF24L01-based link).

Goals

  • Understand the role of the radio module in the control system.
  • Wire the module correctly to the microcontroller.
  • Verify basic send/receive communication between drone and controller.

In-Class Activities

  • Wire the radio module according to a reference diagram.
  • Upload test sketches to send and receive simple data.
  • Observe how range and orientation affect signal strength.

Why This Matters

Without a reliable radio link, you can’t safely control the drone. This week connects physical assembly to real-time control.

Week 20 – Power Wiring & Safety

Students complete power wiring, focusing on safe current paths, protection, and cable management to keep the build robust and tidy.

Goals

  • Route battery power safely to all subsystems.
  • Use appropriate wire gauge, connectors, and insulation.
  • Check for shorts and weak connections before powering up.

In-Class Activities

  • Design and build the main power harness for the drone.
  • Inspect connections visually and with a multimeter for continuity/shorts.
  • Discuss fuse choices, strain relief, and best practices for field repairs.

Why This Matters

Power issues are a common cause of drone failures. Good wiring and safety habits protect both students and hardware.

Week 25 – Bench Flight & Safety Check

Before free flight, students perform thorough bench tests with the fully assembled drone to make sure everything is working together.

Goals

  • Verify that all components are secured and wired correctly.
  • Test full system behavior while the drone is restrained.
  • Confirm safety procedures for starting and stopping the system.

In-Class Activities

  • Perform a full pre-flight checklist (mechanical and electrical).
  • Run throttle and movement tests on a bench rig.
  • Practice emergency shutoffs in simulated problem scenarios.

Why This Matters

Bench checks are a critical gate before flight. Students learn to treat safety as a normal part of engineering work, not an afterthought.

Week 26 – Maiden Flight

The team attempts the first full, untethered flight of the assembled drone in a safe, controlled environment and evaluates its performance.

Goals

  • Conduct a first flight safely and responsibly.
  • Observe how the drone responds to commands and disturbances.
  • Identify tuning or hardware changes needed after initial tests.

In-Class Activities

  • Review the environment and safety rules for flying.
  • Perform short hover and basic maneuver tests.
  • Debrief what went well and what needs adjustment for future flights.

Why This Matters

The maiden flight is a capstone for the build phase. It ties together mechanical, electrical, and software work into a visible success (or a valuable learning moment!).
